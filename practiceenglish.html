General Information

Teacher Name:

Juan David Arroyave Ramirez


School Information

School Name:

Institución Educativa Manuel Uribe Ángel

School's Background:

Special school cycles night modality, "Ciclos Lectivos Integrados Especiales". This school also works in the mornings for Elementary, Middle and High School programs. CLEI is designed to be a flexible model for education of adults or youth that for any reason could not make it to the regular schedule.

CLEI Types:

Elementary (Básica primaria): Grades 1, 2 & 3 (CLEI 1), grades 4 & 5 (CLEI 2). Middle School (Básica secundaria): Grades 6 & 7 (CLEI 3), grades 8 & 9 (CLEI 4). High School (Media): Grade 10 (CLEI 5). and grade 11 (CLEI 6).

Groups Description:

CLEI program is compounded by students coming from great variety of backgrounds, most of these students have suffered of domestic financial difficulties having to work during the day to support household expenses, some of them have experienced sustance disorder, criminal background, indiscipline and adaptation problems. Nonetheless, these students show willingnes to overcome those difficult situations, getting support from the townhall and the municipal secretary of education for their wellness.

Project's Information

Project's Name:

innovative booklet for commonly used regular and irregular verbs.

Project's Description:

Students from CLEI-6 (Grade 11) that are about to get graduated will leave material for the ones that are coming next or they can leave it for themselves. The project consist in preparing an innovative list of commonly used regular and irregular verbs, that can work as a support for understanding better the list of verbs and identifying the ones that are frequently used in their past tense either regular or irregular.

Date:

August 15th/2023

Grade:

CLEI - 4-1 & 3-1

Subject:

English

Schedule:

18:30 - 21:30

Topic:

Review Don't and Doesn't and Household furniture objects

Goal Objective

Understand the use of "Don't and Doesn't" when singular and plural, likewise when it is related to third person. Understand furniture household objects and their locations.

Class Description:

Match review of "Don't" and "Doesn't" on the board, then a "Fill in the gaps" with the right form of Don't and Doesn't, finally translate the sentences. - Review the furniture household objects using the house locations to identify where the object is located at using "There is" and "There are". Then, with a "word search puzzle activity" they will reinforce the objects knowledge. Finally a pictionary game with those words in which students will have to figure out what is the furniture object drawn. For the next session, the guys can create sentences with them using "There is" "There Are" E.g. "There are" dishes in the kitchen/ "There is" a pillow on the Bed.

Pedagogical Reflection:

4-1 CLEI Students were responsive to the class, however, they have huge lacks of vocabulary being so difficult for them to understand the session. Notwithstanding, they focused on the activity performing accurately and asking when needed. I noticed that some of the students get distracted so easy. 3-1 CLEI students Students behave really good, they took part of the activities proposed, the class was clean, no novelties at all.
It is important to identify associations, factors that can be affecting students concentrations, those types of distractors affect their learning process as stated by John Medina in his book: "Brain Rules: 12 Principles for Surviving and Thriving at Work, Home, and School." Medina, J. (2008). Brain Rules: 12 Principles for Surviving and Thriving at Work, Home, and School. Pear Press.

Date:

Tuesday, August 29th /2023

Grade:

CLEI 4-1 & 3-1

Subject:

English

Schedule:

18:30 - 21:30

Topic:

How Many - Introduction of prepositions of place

Goal Objective

Introduce the topic of "how many" with a workshop, finish the furniture household objects topic.

Class Description:

Students will identify the count of tangible objects on the worshop given, they will see some sets on it to figure out and some of them to draw, paint or write according the total of objects listed there. For the written part they will use "there is and there are" and Finish the furniture household objects with a workshop, name the picture in english. Then, introduce the topic of prepositions of place (on, above, below, in, between) using there is and there are.

Pedagogical Reflection:

CLEI 4-1 Students behave really well, they worked on the proposed workshop learning the critical concepts about "How Many" when referring to countable things. CLEI 3-1 Students started the class so distracted, making noise, talking to each other, throwing things, it was difficult to recover their attention, the teacher had to call the coordinator to bring the order back again. After the coordinator scold on them, they started to behave prudently paying attention t the class and teacher's instructions.
CLEI 3-1 had 1 student misssing, while, CLEI 4-1 had like 5 students missing, so, that way it is really hard because students are getting behind.
Managing students behavior is still a challenge because most of their triggers are comming their own psychological aspects as stated on Glasser, W. (1990). That author believes that students have negative behavior whhen they feel their needs are not fulfilled, so, the teacher needs to create a quality school environment where students feel involved in the activities, but this turn difficult when the school does not have enough resources. Moreover, when students don't want to cooperate because of reluctancy. Glasser, W. (1990). The Quality School: Managing Students Without Coercion. HarperCollins.

Schedule:

Topic:

Present Perfect / Prepositions and articles.

Goal Objective

Understand Present Perfect/ Introduction to Past Perfect - Worshop in pairs working the article and preposition "in and at"

Class Description:

Explain the use of have and has. Introduce the topic of had. Workshop to apply the use of has and have. Some of them interchangable with had.Fixing sentences to work with locations understanding the preopositions in and at.

Hence, here is where the effective interventions need to take action on.Starting from this scenario, it is important for the institution to be severe with the assistance for those students that hardly ever assit.
